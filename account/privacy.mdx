NineStyle is built on the principle that your wardrobe is yours. Every item you add, every outfit you build, and every style preference you set belongs to you — and you decide who can see it. The privacy controls in this section give you precise, granular authority over your profile visibility, individual wardrobe items, community sharing, and the data NineStyle collects to power its AI features. You can adjust any of these settings at any time from **Account → Settings → Privacy**.

## Profile visibility

Your profile visibility setting controls what other NineStyle members — and the wider internet — can see when they visit your style page.

<Accordion title="Public">
  Your profile, style bio, follower count, and any outfits you have not individually marked as private are visible to everyone, including people who are not logged in to NineStyle. Search engines may index your public profile. Choose **Public** if you want to grow a following or participate fully in the NineStyle community.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Friends only">
  Your profile and outfits are visible only to NineStyle members you follow back (mutual follows). Visitors who do not follow you see only your display name and avatar. This is a good middle ground between community participation and keeping your style choices relatively private.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Private">
  Your profile, wardrobe, and outfits are completely hidden from other users. You can still browse the community feed and follow other members, but nothing on your profile is visible to anyone other than you. Choose **Private** if you want all the benefits of NineStyle's AI tools without any public presence.
</Accordion>

To change your profile visibility, go to **Settings → Privacy → Profile Visibility**, select your preferred option, and select **Save**.

## Wardrobe and outfit privacy

Beyond your overall profile setting, you can control the visibility of individual wardrobe items and outfits.

**Setting individual items to private**
Open any item in your wardrobe and select the **Privacy** toggle in the item detail panel. Items set to **Private** are hidden from your profile, outfit shares, and the community feed — even if your overall profile is set to Public.

**Setting entire collections to private**
Go to your **Collections** page, open the collection you want to hide, and select **Edit Collection → Visibility → Private**. All items within that collection are hidden from public view while the collection remains private.

**Hiding outfits from the community feed**
When saving or editing an outfit in the Outfit Builder, locate the **Visibility** option in the save dialog and select **Private** or **Friends Only**. Alternatively, open any existing outfit from your Outfit Library, select **Edit**, and change the visibility setting there. Hidden outfits still appear in your personal Outfit Library and calendar — they simply will not appear in the public community feed or on your public profile.

## Data sharing preferences

**Analytics opt-out**
NineStyle collects anonymized usage data — such as which features you use and how frequently — to improve the product. To opt out of this analytics collection, go to **Settings → Privacy → Data Sharing** and turn off **Share anonymized usage analytics**. Opting out does not affect the quality of your personal AI recommendations.

**Personalization data controls**
NineStyle's AI recommendation engine learns from your wardrobe contents, outfit history, and interaction patterns. To pause personalization data collection, go to **Settings → Privacy → Data Sharing** and turn off **Use my activity to improve recommendations**. Your existing recommendations continue to function, but the AI will no longer update its model based on new activity. You can re-enable this at any time to resume learning.

## Your data rights

You have the right to access, export, and delete all personal data associated with your NineStyle account.

**Exporting your data**
NineStyle lets you download a complete copy of your data in **JSON** (full structured data) or **CSV** (spreadsheet-compatible) format. The export includes your wardrobe item catalog, outfit history, style preferences, follower and following lists, and account metadata.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Privacy Settings">
    Go to **Account → Settings → Privacy** and scroll to the **Your Data** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Export My Data">
    Select the **Export My Data** button to open the export dialog.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your format">
    Select **JSON** for a complete, structured data file or **CSV** for a spreadsheet-friendly format. You can request both formats in a single export.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Request the export">
    Select **Request Export**. NineStyle begins compiling your data archive. For most accounts this takes under 10 minutes; large accounts with extensive wardrobe histories may take up to 24 hours.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download from your email">
    When your archive is ready, NineStyle sends a secure download link to your account email address. The link is valid for 7 days. Select the link to download a ZIP file containing your data.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Deleting your account**
You can permanently delete your NineStyle account from **Settings → Privacy → Delete Account**. Before confirming, NineStyle prompts you to download your data export if you have not already done so.

<Warning>
  Account deletion is permanent and irreversible. All your wardrobe items, outfits, style history, follower connections, and account data are immediately and permanently erased from NineStyle's servers. Deleted data cannot be recovered by you or by NineStyle support. If you are unsure, consider setting your profile to **Private** or pausing your Pro subscription instead.
</Warning>

<Note>
  For full details on how NineStyle collects, uses, and protects your personal information, read the [NineStyle Privacy Policy](https://ninestyle.com/privacy). The Privacy Policy is updated whenever our data practices change, and you will be notified by email of any material changes.
</Note>
